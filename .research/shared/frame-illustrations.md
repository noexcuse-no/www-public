## Frame Metaphor Framework

Each frame maps to a discipline and a workplace metaphor. All scenarios place the metaphor inside an office setting.

| Frame | Discipline | Workplace Metaphor | Color Accent |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Struktur** | Economy | Office is A FACTORY | Navy Blue |
| **Mennesker** | Psychology | Office is A FAMILY | Golden Yellow |
| **Påvirknng** | Politics | Office is A JUNGLE | Hunter Green |
| **Identitet** | Cultural Anthropology | Office is A THEATER | Deep Wine |

**Concept per frame:**
| Frame | Concept | Office Activity in Metaphor Setting |
|-------|---------|-------------------------------------|
| Struktur | Five people at a structured meeting in a factory-styled office | Following a structured agenda process at a large board |
| Mennesker | Five people in supportive discussion in a living-room-styled office | Sitting in a supportive circle on comfortable seating |
| Påvirknng | Five people in debate in a jungle-clearing-styled office | Standing in an open forum formation with energized discussion |
| Identitet | Five people on a theater-styled stage | One presenting at a podium, others in audience seats |

## Scenario Specifications

### STRUKTUR (Office is A Factory)

#### Hovedelementer — Central and Surrounding

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Central** | Balanced structure | Five workers at a well-organized assembly line. Every station delivers on time. No waste. | A factory floor where every machine serves a purpose |
| **Surrounding 1** | Roles and responsibilities | Five workers at workstations, each with their own tools. Clear job descriptions on the wall. No overlap. | Assembly line where each station knows its function |
| **Surrounding 2** | Goals and coordination | A production chart showing boxes moving from station to station. Each person delivers to the next precisely. | Conveyor belt in synchronized tempo |
| **Surrounding 3** | Processes and rules | A supervisor following a flowchart step by step. No steps skipped. Efficient. | Standard operating procedure |

#### Utfordringer — Four Challenges

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Challenge 1** | Unclear role definition | Two workers reaching for the same tool. Neither knows if it's theirs. | Two stations claiming the same machine |
| **Challenge 2** | Goal conflicts | Three workers pushing boxes in different directions. The line jams. | Assembly line running in opposing directions |
| **Challenge 3** | Coordination problems | Five workers with separate workstations, none know how the line connects. | Factory floor without a floor plan |
| **Challenge 4** | Bureaucratic burden | One worker buried under a stack of forms and procedures. | Conveyor belt clogged with paperwork |

### MENNESKER (Office is A Family)

#### Hovedelementer — Central and Surrounding

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Central** | Servant leadership | A manager kneeling to help an employee with their work. The team watches approvingly. | Parent serving the family before themselves |
| **Surrounding 1** | Trust and safety | An employee sharing a mistake with their manager, who responds with understanding. | Family member welcomed home without judgment |
| **Surrounding 2** | Motivation and engagement | A team working on a project with energy and enthusiasm. Ideas flowing freely. | Family building something together on a weekend |
| **Surrounding 3** | Development and growth | A senior employee mentoring a junior. Knowledge transferred willingly. | Parent teaching a child to ride a bike |

#### Utfordringer — Four Challenges

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Challenge 1** | Lack of psychological safety | One employee sitting silent in a meeting while others talk over them. | Family dinner where one member never speaks |
| **Challenge 2** | Empty values | Mission statement on the wall, but employees ignoring it in practice. | Family portrait that doesn't match reality |
| **Challenge 3** | Silos | Two departments working separately, never sharing information. | Family members eating in separate rooms |
| **Challenge 4** | Too much volume, too little meaning | Employees buried in emails and meetings, no time to ask "why". | Family talking endlessly without listening |

### PÅVIRKNING (Office is A Jungle)

#### Hovedelementer — Central and Surrounding

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Central** | Constructive power use | Five executives around a conference table, decision made collaboratively. Power serves the outcome. | Hunting party where the pack takes down prey together |
| **Surrounding 1** | Power and influence | A senior leader stepping back, letting others lead. The team follows because they choose to. | Alpha wolf that eats after the pack |
| **Surrounding 2** | Interests and alliances | Two managers forming an alliance on a project. A third watches carefully. | Two predators sharing a watering hole |
| **Surrounding 3** | Conflict and negotiation | Two departments in disagreement, working through a mediator to find a solution. | Two predators negotiating territory |

#### Utfordringer — Four Challenges

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Challenge 1** | Unconscious power use | A leader who doesn't realize their decisions control the room. Others wait for their signal. | Lion unaware that the herd moves when it walks |
| **Challenge 2** | Coalitions | Two teams working against each other, blocking initiatives. | Competing packs guarding opposite sides of the forest |
| **Challenge 3** | Escalation | A disagreement in a meeting that escalates to shouting. Body language becomes hostile. | Fire spreading through dry brush |
| **Challenge 4** | Power loss | A former leader now isolated, their office empty, others passing by without acknowledgment. | Old predator displaced from its territory |

---

### IDENTITET (Office is A Theater)

#### Hovedelementer — Central and Surrounding

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Central** | Tribal Stage Four | Five employees on a stage delivering a presentation. Audience clapping. The team owns the spotlight. | Theater troupe performing to a standing ovation |
| **Surrounding 1** | Culture and values | New employees learning the company's founding story from a veteran. The ritual repeated. | Actors learning the company's classic lines |
| **Surrounding 2** | Meaning and identity | An all-hands meeting where the CEO shares the company's vision. Everyone aligned. | Director explaining the play's meaning to the cast |
| **Surrounding 3** | Rituals and symbols | The annual awards ceremony where a trophy is passed from person to person. Tradition. | Cast passing a prop as a symbol of continuity |

#### Utfordringer — Four Challenges

| Type | Element | Office Situation | Metaphor |
|------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Challenge 1** | Individual dominance | One executive taking all the credit for a team effort. The others in the background. | Lead actor stealing the scene from the ensemble |
| **Challenge 2** | Meaningless rituals | The weekly meeting where everyone goes through the motions, no one knows why. | Cast performing on an empty stage |
| **Challenge 3** | Historylessness | A company with no founding story, no milestones celebrated. New hires feel lost. | Theater with no scripts or programs |
| **Challenge 4** | Change fatigue | A reform initiative shown on a wall chart, all arrows pointing down. Everyone has given up. | Play that has been rewritten so many times no one believes in it anymore |

## Lessons Learned About Metaphors (Session 2026-05-24)

### Key Insight: Environment ≠ Activity

The frame metaphors describe the **environment** the office activity happens in, not the activity itself.

**Pattern:** Office activity + Metaphor setting = Frame illustration concept

| Frame | Metaphor | Environment | Office Activity |
|-------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Struktur | Factory | "industrial factory hall" | Following a structured agenda process |
| Mennesker | Family | "cozy living room setting" | Sitting in supportive circle discussion |
| Påvirknng | Jungle | "jungle clearing setting" | Standing in open forum debate |
| Identitet | Theater | "theater stage setting" | Presenting at podium to audience |

### Style Consistency is Critical

Early frame hero generations failed because:
1. Over-specified environment details pushed toward photo-realistic rendering
2. Missing explicit style anchors ("simple shapes, clean lines, geometric shapes")

**Successful style template (Style 2: Professional Abstract):**
```
Professional abstract illustration for leadership frame banner,
clean minimal Scandinavian style, showing [subject],
simple shapes, clean lines, geometric shapes, [frame color] and neutral tones,
clean white background, symbolic imagery only, no text, 16:9 horizontal format
```

### Four Distinct Illustration Styles

| Style | Use for | Key phrase |
|-------|---------|------------|
| Style 1: Hero Illustrations | Landing page heroes (forest/nature) | "minimalist Nordic style" |
| Style 2: Professional Abstract | Benefit banners, frame heroes, process steps | "clean minimal Scandinavian style" |
| Style 3: Section Illustrations | Frame section images (hovedelementer/utfordringer) | "Clean minimal infographic" |
| Style 4: CTA Illustrations | Call-to-action sections | "clipboard with checkmarks" |

### Color Accuracy Matters

Frame accent colors must match the spec exactly:

| Frame | Correct Color | Previously Used (Wrong) |
|-------|--------------|-------------------------|
| Struktur | Navy Blue (#2A4D6E) | — |
| Mennesker | Golden Yellow (#D4A836) | — |
| Påvirknng | Hunter Green (#355E3B) | amber |
| Identitet | Deep Wine (#8E0D3C) | purple |

### View Angle Should Not Be Specified

For frame hero banners, view angle should be decided dynamically by scenario rather than specifying "seen from behind" or "facing camera". The AI can determine appropriate perspective based on the described activity.

## Prompt Generation Checklist

When generating any frame image, verify:

- [ ] Using correct style template for image type
- [ ] Frame color is correct per frame (see color table above)
- [ ] "no text" included
- [ ] Aspect ratio is 16:9 (landscape)
- [ ] Subject describes office situation with metaphorical setting
- [ ] No over-specified environment details (keep prompts short and descriptive)
- [ ] Include "simple shapes, clean lines, geometric shapes" for Professional Abstract style
- [ ] No faces or identifiable people
- [ ] No Norwegian or English words in prompt
- [ ] No brand names or logos
