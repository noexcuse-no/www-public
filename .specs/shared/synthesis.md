### Tension Pairs

| Pair | Tension | Resolution for 60:2 |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| **Pfeffer ↔ Blanchard & Barrett** | Power acquisition vs. servant leadership | Both perspectives are valid; the 60:2 diagnoses *which* orientation actually exists in the leadership culture — and whether it's fit for purpose |
| **Hubbard ↔ Logan** | Quantitative measurement vs. qualitative stage assessment | Hubbard's methods can strengthen Logan's model through calibration and Bayesian classification; the 60:2 uses both |
| **Pfeffer ↔ Logan** | Individual power (Stage Three) vs. tribal collaboration (Stage Four/Five) | The 60:2 can detect whether the culture is stuck in "lone warrior" mode or has progressed to collaborative tribal leadership |

## How the Sources Support the 60:2 Philosophy

### "No scoring, no right/wrong"
- **Bolman & Deal:** No frame is universally correct — context determines what works
- **Hubbard:** All measurement reduces uncertainty; there is no perfect knowledge
- **Logan:** Stages are descriptive, not prescriptive — moving up is not always appropriate

### "Reflection is as important as the report"
- **Bolman & Deal:** Leadership is artistry; the process of reframing is itself valuable
- **Blanchard & Barrett:** The quality of relationships (built through the interview) matters as much as outcomes
- **Hubbard:** Even imperfect measurement generates value if it informs decisions

### "Comparative insight over absolute scores"
- **Bolman & Deal:** Compare across frames; compare across leaders; no single number captures organizational reality
- **Logan:** Compare language patterns across leaders to detect cultural stage alignment/misalignment
- **Pfeffer:** Compare power distribution across the leadership group

### "Qualitative method as counterpoint to AI/automation"
- **Hubbard:** Quantification is valuable but must be decision-focused — not automated for its own sake
- **Bolman & Deal:** Artistry, intuition, and reflective practice cannot be replaced by algorithms

## Recommended Reference Workflow

When processing Ledelse 60:2 data, follow this sequence:

1. **Frame classification** — classify each response/finding per Bolman & Deal's four frames
2. **Primary source lookup** — for each frame, consult the relevant dedicated brief:
   - Structural → Hubbard
   - HR → Blanchard & Barrett
   - Political → Pfeffer
   - Symbolic → Logan
3. **Stage classification** — for any cultural/language findings, consult Logan's tribal stages
4. **Measurement validation** — for any quantitative claims, consult Hubbard's EVPI framework
5. **Integration** — for holistic analysis, return to Bolman & Deal's multiframe thinking
6. **Cross-reference** — use the lookup vectors above to connect findings across sources
